Transforming Agricultural Waste into Clean Industrial Fuel

Karagreen converts agricultural residue into high-grade biomass pellets, providing scalable and reliable alternatives to coal for Indian industries.

Why Biomass Pellets Matter for India

The Energy Challenge

Indian industries face rising emission and cost pressures due to heavy reliance on coal. At the same time, vast quantities of agricultural waste are openly burned, releasing carbon and particulates. This dual challenge requires scalable clean energy solutions.

The Biomass Solution

Biomass pellets provide a consistent, high-energy alternative to coal. Standardized pellet fuel enables predictable performance while reducing emissions and addressing agricultural waste disposal challenges.

Understanding Biomass Pellets

  • Raw Material

    Produced from agricultural residues such as rice husk, groundnut shells, and cotton stalks, processed to achieve uniform fuel characteristics.

  • Dense Energy Form

    Pelletization compresses loose biomass into compact cylinders with bulk density of 600–700 kg/m³, enabling efficient storage and transport.

  • Energy Value

    Gross calorific value typically ranges between 3,800–4,200 kcal/kg, depending on feedstock and moisture control.

Key Advantages Over Coal

  • Lower ash content for easier handling and disposal
  • Reduced sulfur and particulate emissions
  • Carbon-neutral combustion aligned with ESG goals
  • Greater price stability versus fossil fuels
  • Safer storage without spontaneous combustion risks

Our Manufacturing Process

  1. Sourcing: Agricultural residues procured from verified networks.
  2. Drying: Moisture reduced to optimal 8–12% for pelletization.
  3. Size Reduction: Uniform particle sizing via hammer mills.
  4. Pelletization: High-pressure compression using natural lignin binding.
  5. Cooling & Screening: Stabilization and removal of fines.

Industrial Applications

Industrial Boilers

Used for steam and process heat in textile, food processing, and chemical industries.

Thermal Power Plants

Co-firing with coal to reduce emissions while maintaining grid stability.

Cement & Steel

High-temperature processes use pellets as auxiliary fuel to reduce coal dependence.

Sustainability Impact

Biomass combustion releases CO₂ recently absorbed by plants, unlike fossil fuels that release ancient carbon. When sourced sustainably, biomass energy maintains atmospheric carbon balance.

  • Prevents open-field burning and air pollution
  • Reduces greenhouse gas and particulate emissions
  • Creates rural income and employment
  • Supports a circular economy
